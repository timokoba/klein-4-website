Automated Cold Backups

Offline copies of your data remain secure in case of any cyber intrusion, even if the hacker gains unrestricted root-level access on all networked devices. Cold backups ensure data survivability against insider threats and sophisticated state-sponsored actors.

Klein 4 NAS transforms cold backup creation from a labour-intensive and error-prone task into an automatic and continuous process.

Continuity of Business Operations

Ransomware and human error alike can bring business to a standstill by making critical data unavailable. When key files are deleted or otherwise made inaccessible, teams scramble to recover or recreate them.

Such troubles are prevented by restoring files from a physically air-gapped but frequently updated copy, ensuring Business Continuity and robustly mitigating any unexpected IT problems, no matter the source.

Offline Disk Controller

The device dynamically manages the physical access to the data storage using multiplexers configured according to the policy enforced by an auxiliary computer, called Offline Disk Controller. Its security is guaranteed due to security domain segmentation and use of data diodes to control the information flow.

Tamper-Proofing

The device features a range of tamper-evident and tamper-resistant hardening, from sensors embedded in the casing and security tags, through audio alarm, to phone notifications on admin access.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the target audience?

The device is built specifically for European SMEs and shaped by over 100 in-depth customer interviews, with particular emphasis on engineering companies and creative agencies.

How does it integrate into our existing workflow?

It integrates seamlessly with your existing workflows and systems, including Microsoft 365, enabling your team to maintain established processes while the device operates unobtrusively in the background.

Can I access my files remotely?

The NAS can be accessed remotely via a secure, end-to-end encrypted proxy connection through the K4OS platform, allowing you to reach your files from outside the office.

What happens in case of a security incident?

The NAS supports role-based permissions at both user and group level, allowing you to precisely control who can view, edit, or share specific folders. All administrative access is logged, and suspicious activity is flagged, helping you detect, contain, and respond to potential security incidents.
